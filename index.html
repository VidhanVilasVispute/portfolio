A product thinker from Cohort-22 with a sharp eye for user behavior gaps and data-driven decision making. This case study tackles one of the most challenging PM problems: building engagement in a low-frequency purchase category.

The research involved 48 survey respondents, 10+ qualitative interviews, competitor analysis across 4 brands, and industry reports from Statista, RedSeer, and Deloitte.

"How do you make people open an app for a product they only need once every 2 years? You don't sell eyewear — you sell eye health."

Risks & Trade-offs

What Could
Go Wrong

Honest risk assessment with mitigations — because good product thinking anticipates failure before it happens.

HIGH RISK
Adoption Risk
Users may not perceive value in non-transactional engagement — the app is "just eyewear" in their minds.
Mitigation
Strong onboarding, clear value prop, early rewards that feel immediately worthwhile
HIGH RISK
Habit Formation
Eye care is fundamentally low-frequency — building a daily habit around it fights human nature.
Mitigation
Gamification, streaks, challenges that make eye care feel like a game, not a chore
MED RISK
Data Privacy
Eye health data is sensitive — users may resist sharing prescription details with a commercial platform.
Mitigation
Transparent privacy policy, opt-in data collection, clear user control over their data
MED RISK
Competitive Response
Offline opticians have trust advantages that digital features can't easily replicate.
Mitigation
Leverage digital convenience and personalization as moat — opticians can't match AI recommendations
MED RISK
Engagement Fatigue
Over-notification could cause users to disable alerts or uninstall the app entirely.
Mitigation
Smart notification timing, user preference controls, ML-based optimal send-time
TRADE-OFF
Complexity vs. Speed
The comprehensive solution takes longer to build — phased rollout risks losing momentum.
Decision
MVP approach — ship core loops first, validate, then expand based on real user behavior
